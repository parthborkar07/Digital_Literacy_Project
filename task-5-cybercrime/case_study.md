# **Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud**
# **What the Crime Is**
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of financial cybercrime where attackers deceive victims into unauthorized fund transfers using mobile payment apps. Unlike traditional hacking, this crime primarily relies on Social Engineering, exploiting a user’s lack of technical familiarity with how digital "Request Money" features work.

# **How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)**

**The Hook**: The fraudster finds a target on online marketplaces (like OLX) or social media. They pose as a buyer interested in an item the victim is selling or as a customer service agent.

**The Deception**: The attacker claims they want to send an "advance payment." Instead of sending money, they send a "Collect Request" via a UPI app (like GPay or PhonePe).

**The Trigger**: The attacker tells the victim, "I have sent the money; you just need to click 'Pay' and enter your UPI PIN to receive it."

**The Theft**: Since a UPI PIN is only required to send money, not receive it, the victim inadvertently authorizes a debit from their own account the moment they enter their PIN.

# **Who is Usually Targeted**
Targets often include students, elderly individuals, or small-scale online sellers who may be distracted or unfamiliar with the specific interface of payment apps. Attackers also target people looking for "too-good-to-be-true" deals or urgent customer support.

# **Consequences**
The immediate consequence is the total loss of funds from the linked bank account. Because UPI transactions are instantaneous, recovering the money is extremely difficult. Beyond financial loss, victims often experience significant psychological stress and a loss of trust in digital banking systems.
